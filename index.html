What is Space?

  • Space is the area that is directly outside of Earth's atmosphere.
  • Space begins about 100km (62 miles) above earth at a point known as the Kármán Line.
  • Outer space refers to anything outside of Earth's atmosphere. This includes planets, stars, black holes, asteroids, comets, galaxies and lots more!
  • Space mostly consists of Hydrogen and Helium Plasma which is permeated by electromagnetic radiation, cosmic rays, neutrinos, magnetic fields and dust.
  • Space is a near perfect vacuum with extremely low pressure.
  • Its around 13.7 billion years old according to Nasa.
  • Space has no air which means a living thing can not survive without high-tech spacesuits to travel there.
  • The first living thing to enter space was a dog named Laika on the 3rd of November 1957.
  • Yuri Gagarin was the first human in space on April 12th 1961. He circled earth at a speed of 27,400 km/h. The total flight lasted 108 minutes.
  • 95% of Space is invisible meaning that humans only know and see 5% of the universe.
